Welcome to FOSS@KIIT

FOSS@KIIT is the open source society of KIIT University. It is a volunteer driven society that focuses on free and open source software (FOSS), open standards, open technologies, and open content. The goal of this society is to promote awareness, usage, and development of FOSS in KIIT University.

The FOSS community of KIIT consists of students and alumni of the university who come together on this platform to share and exchange their knowledge regarding FOSS and collaborate on open source projects.

Email fosskiit@fosskiit.org or connect to #fosskiit to join us.

What is FOSS?

FOSS stands for free and open source software. It is the union of two philosophies: free software movement and open source initiative. The two philosophies have subtle differences in their goals but are mostly similar in their essence. They both promote the idea that computer users should be free to use computer programs without any restrictions, study their source code, redistribute them, and modify them.

This philosophy promotes a culture of cooperation between software developers as well as users. Knowledge, in the form of source code, documentation, and improvements to software flows freely between computer users.

Why FOSS?

Free and open source software is becoming increasingly important in the technology industry. Open source tools drive innovation in the industry today. In fact, the Internet, the leading compilers, databases, security protocols, etc. are all based on open source technologies and open collaboration.

Given the extensive use of open source technologies in the industry, students and professionals skilled in open source are high in demand.

One of the best things about being involved in open source is that your work does not stay locked in a particular organization. As you move from one organization to another, your work remains with the open source community and you. Since your work is released under an open source license, the legal framework around the license ensures that your work is always accessible to you and others who may benefit from your work. The fact that potential users, collaborators, and employers can reach your work easily and unencumbered earns you street cred in the technology community.
